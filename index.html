Research

Athena: Attribution-Driven Skill Learning for Small LMs

(2026–present, designer & lead; in collaboration with an industry research group)

Can a 4B model learn to diagnose why it fails — and get better at diagnosing over time? Athena trains attribution into the weights of Qwen3.5-4B: the model reads its own failure traces, produces structured skill entries, and each skill must pass a causal gate (held-out Δ under a specified retrieval regime) before entering the library.

Selected results so far:

  • Attribution is trainable and surgically improvable: parse rate 34.7%→99.7%, false-production 48.3%→0%, teacher-inherited misdiagnoses eliminated (6→0) across three SFT iterations
  • A zero-annotation verification signal: attribution correctness graded by the base model’s actual behavior after weight updates (87.8% prediction accuracy)
  • Skill value is not intrinsic: the same skill measured Δ = +0.06 in one retrieval-competition regime and −0.24 in another — a finding with consequences for how skill libraries are evaluated
  • Full experimental discipline: pre-registered criteria, provenance tracking, contamination auditing; a pre-registered anomaly gate caught a silent sampling bug before it could contaminate downstream training

Technical report available on request. Workshop paper in preparation.
